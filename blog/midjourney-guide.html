Midjourney Guide: AI Image Generation Explained

A beginner-friendly guide · 5 min read

Midjourney is an AI tool that generates images from text descriptions, known as "prompts." Type a description of what you want to see — a scene, a character, a style of art — and Midjourney creates original images based on your words within seconds.

It's widely used by artists, designers, marketers, and hobbyists to create concept art, illustrations, social media visuals, and creative experiments that would otherwise take hours to draw or design by hand.

How does it work?

Midjourney uses a type of AI model trained on a huge collection of images and their descriptions. When you give it a prompt, it generates new images that match the style, subject, and mood you described — without copying any single existing image directly.

What can you create with it?

  • Concept art and illustrations for stories, games, or projects
  • Logos, icons, and branding visuals (as a starting point)
  • Social media graphics and thumbnails
  • Product mockups and design inspiration
  • Personal art experiments in different styles, from realistic to fantasy

How to get started

  • Create an account on the Midjourney platform
  • Open the image generation interface and type a description of the image you want
  • Add details like art style, lighting, color palette, or mood for better results
  • Generate multiple versions and pick the one you like, or refine your prompt and try again

Tips for better prompts

Good results start with clear, descriptive prompts. A few tips:

  • Describe the subject, setting, and style separately — for example, "a fox sitting in a snowy forest, watercolor style, soft lighting"
  • Mention a specific art style or artist's technique if you have one in mind
  • Experiment with different wording — small changes can produce very different results
  • Use reference images alongside text prompts when supported, for more control

Final thoughts

Midjourney makes high-quality image creation accessible to anyone, regardless of artistic skill. The more you experiment with prompts, the better you'll get at turning your ideas into striking visuals.
